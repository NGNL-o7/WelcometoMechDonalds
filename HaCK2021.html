Team Member's Bio & Experiences
Steven Kong (MechE)
I came into this project with the skills provided during basic training and HaCK training.
Prior to those training I had minimal experince in arduino and circuitry with little to
no experience with Solidworks, Physically assembling parts, or website development. During
the HaCK I was able to develop my understanding in assembling parts with screws, mounts,
and faseners. I was also able to improve my circuitry after burning a frew wires and improve
my arduino coding as well as getting a better understanding of bluetooth communication.
During the time I workded on the website I was able to explore new formating techniques
to improve the style of the website. HaCK overall was a bit of a struggle as I tried to
figure out why the servo.attach affect the arduino pins, why I burned wire, why my bluetooth
got extremely hot during use, and how to design a proper mount for the servo. Reguardless
HaCK was alot of fun and a great learning experience.
Andrew T. Kunkler (MechE)
My name is Andrew Kunkler and I am excited to be studying Mechanical Engineering at UCLA.
I feel that my greatest skills lie in training, leadership and creative problem solving, and
I hope to leverage these technical skills in project management and systems engineering.
This HAcK competition proved to be a challenge, but I feel so much stronger in areas of Arduino,
coding and CAD, and appreciate the opportunity of trial by fire. While I found the process of piecing
together the car components to be the greatest challenge of all, I also found this to be the most
fun and exciting. I am confident that I have learned a lot, and I look forward to leveraging this wide
range of new skills at UCLA and beyond.
Lein Pasache (MechE)
I am Lein Pasache, a Mechanical Engineer that will be attending UCLA in the fall.
My greatest skill is in practical training and time management. I plan to use these skills in my career
as an engineering for one of the major space companies like NASA or SpaceX. This HAcK competition was
a true challenge for me, but I rose to even surprise myself. The training I had to prepare for the HAcK
really made me strive to be great and made me stronger on the areas of Arduino and CAD. It really showed me
how hard I could work in a short period of time. I love working with my hands and the experience I got in
assembling the RC car. I look forward to using these skills to practice in my studies and further understanding
more complexes skills in the future.
